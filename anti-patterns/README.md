# Anti-Patterns

These metrics often look attractive because they are easy to collect, but they usually produce weak insight or bad incentives.

## Raw chat volume

More prompts do not mean more value. High volume can indicate confusion, poor UX, or repeated failed attempts.

## Lines of code

This rewards verbosity, not better engineering outcomes. It can increase risk and make AI-generated output look falsely productive.

## Story points

Story points are local planning heuristics, not a business value metric. They are too easy to distort once tied to performance.

## Jira hours

Hours logged are not the same as useful throughput or better outcomes. This metric tends to reward theater.

## Number of models deployed

This is delivery activity, not business impact. A company can deploy many models without improving a single important workflow.

## Unverified time-saved claims

Self-reported savings can be directionally useful, but they are too weak to stand alone. Treat them as supporting evidence, not the main KPI.

## Better substitutes

Prefer metrics tied to:

- workflow completion
- customer or stakeholder outcomes
- quality and rework
- review and override behavior
- safe adoption in target workflows
