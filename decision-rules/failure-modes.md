# Failure Modes

These are common ways AI measurement programs look successful before they actually create trustworthy value.

## Adoption theater

The team reports high usage, but the workflow outcome does not improve.

What to do:

- separate usage from value metrics
- inspect whether usage is voluntary and workflow-specific

## Metric gaming

Teams optimize for the reported number rather than the business result.

What to do:

- add paired quality and risk metrics
- rotate review questions from the manager playbook

## Hidden downstream work

The measured workflow gets faster, but extra cleanup appears later in another team or step.

What to do:

- inspect downstream rework and exception handling
- track the full workflow, not only the assisted step

## Weak instrumentation bias

One team appears to outperform others only because it captures fewer exceptions or overrides.

What to do:

- audit instrumentation completeness
- avoid ranking teams before measurement quality is comparable

## Delayed quality damage

Short-term speed improves, but defect, customer, or policy issues emerge later.

What to do:

- use lagging quality reviews
- do not scale on short-term speed data alone

## Manager pressure bias

Employees use AI because they feel required to, not because it helps.

What to do:

- use qualitative review alongside quantitative data
- inspect discard and override behavior
