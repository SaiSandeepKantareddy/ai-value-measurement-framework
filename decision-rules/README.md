# Decision Rules

Measurement is only useful if it changes decisions.

## Pattern: High adoption, low quality

Interpretation:
The workflow is being used, but the AI or process is not producing reliable outcomes.

Recommended action:
- pause broader rollout
- inspect failure modes
- improve guidance, retrieval, or review policy

## Pattern: High speed, low business impact

Interpretation:
The workflow became faster, but the outcome does not matter enough or the wrong metric was chosen.

Recommended action:
- revisit workflow selection
- replace vanity speed metrics with outcome metrics

## Pattern: Low adoption, strong pilot outcomes

Interpretation:
The capability may be valuable, but enablement, UX, or trust is blocking usage.

Recommended action:
- improve onboarding
- tighten workflow fit
- target the teams with the clearest need first

## Pattern: High override rate

Interpretation:
The AI output is not reliable enough, or the UI makes acceptance awkward.

Recommended action:
- inspect override reasons
- segment by workflow and content type
- do not treat high usage as success

## Pattern: Stable value, rising policy exceptions

Interpretation:
The workflow may be scaling faster than governance can support.

Recommended action:
- increase review rigor
- tighten allowed use cases
- define explicit pause thresholds
