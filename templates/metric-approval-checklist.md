# Metric Approval Checklist

Approve a metric only if most of these answers are yes.

## Relevance

- Does the metric tie to a specific workflow?
- Does it reflect business-relevant behavior or outcome?
- Would a leader care about this metric in a decision review?

## Integrity

- Do we have a clear baseline?
- Can the metric be measured consistently?
- Is the source of truth identified?

## Safety

- Could this metric be gamed easily?
- Is there a paired quality or risk metric?
- Could optimizing this metric create harmful behavior?

## Actionability

- Would a movement in this metric change a decision?
- Can the team explain what to do if the metric improves or worsens?
- Is the metric simple enough to interpret without heavy debate?

## Reject metrics that are mostly:

- activity without outcome
- anecdotal without evidence
- easy to inflate
- disconnected from a real workflow
