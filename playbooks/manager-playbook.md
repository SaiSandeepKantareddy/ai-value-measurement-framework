# Manager Playbook

This guide is for managers who need to use the framework in real team operations, not just read about it.

## Your job

As a manager, your role is to:

- choose a workflow worth measuring
- make sure the baseline is real
- review outcomes weekly
- protect the team from bad metrics
- escalate when speed improves but value or quality does not

## What to do first

1. Pick one team workflow with real pain and measurable output.
2. Define how the workflow starts and what counts as complete.
3. Choose one metric each for adoption, speed, quality, and risk.
4. Add throughput or leverage only if the workflow is stable enough to interpret them.
5. Make sure a human reviewer is named for medium- or high-risk workflows.

## What to review every week

- Is adoption happening in the intended workflow or just in adjacent usage?
- Is speed improving against the baseline?
- Is quality holding or improving?
- Are overrides or policy exceptions rising?
- Are people using the system because it helps, or because they were told to?

## Questions to ask in team reviews

- What changed in the workflow this week?
- What metric moved in a meaningful way?
- What does that movement mean for the business?
- What are we worried is being hidden by this metric?
- Should we scale, fix, pause, or keep observing?

## Mistakes to avoid

- treating usage as impact
- rewarding teams for AI activity
- using self-reported time saved as the main proof
- scaling before review coverage is stable
- comparing teams with very different workflow risk levels

## When to escalate

Escalate to your function leader or AI program owner when:

- quality drops while adoption rises
- exception rates increase for two review cycles
- the team cannot explain a metric improvement in business terms
- users are bypassing review requirements
- instrumentation gaps make the scorecard unreliable
